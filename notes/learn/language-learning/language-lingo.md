# Language Lingo

| Term                      | Definition                                                                                                                                                            |
| ------------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Pronoun**               | A word that stands in for a noun (e.g., I, you, he, she, it, we, they).                                                                                               |
| **Personal Pronoun**      | A pronoun that refers to a specific person or thing (e.g., I, you, he, she, it, we, they).                                                                            |
| **Objective Pronoun**     | A pronoun used as the object of a verb or preposition (e.g., me, you, him, her, it, us, them).                                                                        |
| **Reflexive Pronoun**     | A form of a pronoun that refers back to the subject of the sentence (e.g., myself, yourself, himself).                                                                |
| **Possessive Pronoun**    | A pronoun that shows ownership and stands alone in place of a noun (e.g., mine, yours, his, hers, ours, theirs).                                                      |
| **Relative Pronoun**      | A pronoun that introduces a clause giving more information about a noun (e.g., who, whom, whose, which, that).                                                        |
| **Subject**               | The noun or pronoun that performs the action of the verb in a sentence.                                                                                               |
| **Object**                | The noun or pronoun that receives the action of the verb in a sentence.                                                                                               |
| **Plural**                | Refers to more than one person, thing, or idea (e.g., cats, children).                                                                                                |
| **Singular**              | Refers to one person, thing, or idea (e.g., cat, child).                                                                                                              |
| **Prepositions**          | Words that help show the relationship between things.                                                                                                                 |
| **Inversion**             | A change in the normal word order of a sentence — most commonly when the verb is placed before the subject instead of after it. Normally used to indicate a question. |
| **Auxiliary/Helper Verb** | An auxiliary is a helper verb that carries grammar (questions, tense, negation), while the main verb carries meaning.                                                 |
