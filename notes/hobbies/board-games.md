## Nimmt 6

- Players simultaneously play one card face down, then reveal
- Cards are added to the four rows in ascending order
- If a card can't fit in any row (higher than all row ends), player takes a row and its cards count as penalty points
- After 10 rounds, fewest penalty points wins

## Jaipur

### Goal

Earn more points than your opponent by trading goods efficiently across 2 rounds. Win a round by having the most points when 3 good token stacks are empty or the deck runs out.

### Setup

- 2 players
- Shuffle all cards together.
- Deal 5 cards face up to the market.
- Replace 3 of those cards with camels, so the market starts with 3 camels and 2 random cards.
- Deal 5 cards to each player.
- If a player has camels in hand, reveal them and place them in that player's camel herd.
- Put the remaining cards face down as the draw deck.
- Sort goods tokens by type in descending value.
- Place bonus tokens for selling 3, 4, and 5 goods separately.

### Goods

- Expensive goods: diamond, gold, silver
- Cheap goods: cloth, spice, leather
- Camels are not sold; they help with trading and can score a herd bonus.

### On Your Turn

Do exactly 1 action:

1. Take goods
2. Take all camels
3. Trade
4. Sell goods

### 1. Take Goods

- Take 1 non-camel card from the market into your hand.
- Refill the market from the deck.
- Hand limit is 7 cards.
- Camels in your herd do not count toward the hand limit.

### 2. Take All Camels

- If the market has camels, take all camels from the market and place them into your herd.
- Refill the market from the deck back up to 5 cards.

### 3. Trade

- Trade cards from your hand and/or camel herd for the same number of cards in the market.
- You must take at least 2 cards from the market when trading.
- You cannot trade away cards and take camels in the same trade.
- You can use camels from your herd as part of the payment.
- Your hand limit of 7 still applies after the trade.

### 4. Sell Goods

- Sell 1 type of goods from your hand.
- Take the top goods tokens for that type equal to the number of cards sold.
- Sold cards go to a discard pile.

Special rule for expensive goods:

- Diamond, gold, and silver must be sold in sets of at least 2 cards.

Bonus tokens:

- Sell 3 cards: take 1 random 3-card bonus token.
- Sell 4 cards: take 1 random 4-card bonus token.
- Sell 5 or more cards: take 1 random 5-card bonus token.

### Round End

The round ends immediately if either happens:

- 3 types of goods tokens are empty
- The draw deck is empty and the market cannot be refilled

### Scoring

- Add points from all goods tokens collected.
- Add points from bonus tokens.
- The player with more camels in their herd gets the camel bonus token.
- Higher total wins the round and takes a Seal of Excellence.

### Winning The Game

- The game ends when 1 player has 2 Seals of Excellence.

### Quick Tips

- Taking camels can set up bigger trades later.
- Selling cheap goods early can deny your opponent tempo.
- Holding expensive goods too long can backfire if the round ends suddenly.
- Large sales are strong because bonus tokens swing scores.
