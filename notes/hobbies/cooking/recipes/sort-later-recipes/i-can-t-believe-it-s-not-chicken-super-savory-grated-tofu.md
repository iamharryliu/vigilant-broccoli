# I Can’t Believe It’s Not Chicken (Super-Savory Grated Tofu)

## Ingredients

- **Rice**
  - 190g to 285g uncooked white or brown rice (or 425g to 650g cooked white or brown rice)

- **For the Tofu**
  - 280g to 340g super-firm tofu
  - 22ml neutral-flavored oil of choice
  - 4 scallions, sliced at an angle (reserve dark green tops for garnish)
  - 1 to 2 Thai chiles or 1 small serrano pepper, thinly sliced (optional)
  - 3 garlic cloves, thinly sliced
  - 1 tablespoon roasted black or white sesame seeds

- **For the Sauce**
  - 52g tamari or soy sauce
  - 15ml Chinese black vinegar
  - 5g organic cane sugar, pure maple syrup or agave nectar
  - 15g gochugaru (Korean chile flakes)
  - 15ml toasted sesame oil

- **For Serving**
  - Handful of cilantro leaves and tender stems, roughly chopped

## Instructions

- **Cook the rice** using your preferred method or use leftover cooked rice.
- **Wrap the tofu** in a thin dish towel and gently squeeze to remove some water. Grate it using the large holes of a box grater.
- In a large nonstick skillet, **heat the oil** over medium-high heat. Add scallions, chiles (if using), and garlic. Cook until garlic is slightly golden and scallions are softened, about 2 minutes.
- Add the grated tofu to the pan and **toss to coat** in the oil. Cook undisturbed for 2 minutes, then stir. Cook, stirring every 2 minutes, until tofu is golden brown in some spots, 10 to 14 minutes.
- **Make the sauce:** In a small bowl, whisk together tamari, vinegar, sugar, gochugaru, and sesame oil until well combined.
- Pour the sauce into the pan and stir to **evenly coat** the tofu. Cook for 1 minute. Remove from heat and sprinkle with sesame seeds.
- **Serve over cooked rice** and top with reserved scallion tops and cilantro. Store leftovers in an airtight container in the fridge for 4 to 5 days.
