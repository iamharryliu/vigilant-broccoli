# Vegan Dal Makhani

## Ingredients

- Pulses
  - 1 cup (205g) whole urad dal (black gram)
  - ¼ cup (44g) dried kidney beans
  - 1 teaspoon baking soda
- Whole Spices
  - 1½ tablespoons grapeseed oil (or neutral oil)
  - 1 cinnamon stick (2 to 3 inches)
  - 4 green cardamom pods (seeds only)
  - 3 whole cloves
  - 1 bay leaf
  - 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
- Aromatics and Ground Spices
  - 1 medium-large red onion, very finely diced
  - 1-inch piece fresh ginger, minced or grated
  - 4 garlic cloves, finely chopped
  - 2 tablespoons tomato paste
  - ⅛ teaspoon nutmeg (preferably freshly grated)
  - 1 teaspoon coriander
  - ½ teaspoon Indian red chile powder (1 teaspoon for a spicier version)
  - 2 teaspoons kosher salt
  - Freshly cracked black pepper
  - 2 medium tomatoes (230-280g), diced
- Other ingredients
  - 3½ to 4 cups (840-960ml) liquid (water + bean cooking liquid)
  - ½ cup (120ml) cashew cream or full-fat coconut milk
  - 1 cup (12g) cilantro leaves and tender stems, chopped
  - 1 tablespoon lemon juice, plus more as needed
  - ½ to 1 teaspoon organic cane sugar (as needed)

### Dhungar method (for smokiness; optional)
- 1 to 2-inch piece of lump charcoal
- ½ teaspoon neutral-flavored oil

### Tadka
- 3 tablespoons (42g) vegan butter
- 1-inch piece ginger, peeled and cut into matchsticks (optional)
- 1 tablespoon kasoori methi, crushed with your hands
- 1 teaspoon garam masala
- ½ teaspoon Indian red chile powder

### Cashew Cream (optional)
- ½ cup (70g) raw cashews
- 6 tablespoons (90ml) water, more as needed
- 1 tablespoon freshly squeezed lemon juice
- ½ teaspoon kosher salt

## Instructions

- Rinse the whole urad dal and kidney beans; soak with baking soda in cold water for 8 hours or overnight.
- Drain and rinse until water runs clear. Cook the pulses with water and ½ teaspoon salt; boil for 10 minutes, simmer for 80-90 minutes until soft.
- Drain, reserving cooking liquid. Mash the lentils and beans to desired consistency.
- Heat oil in a deep sauté pan; add whole spices and toast until aromatic.
- Add the onions, cook until lightly browned, then add ginger and garlic; sauté for 1 minute.
- Stir in tomato paste, nutmeg, coriander, red chile powder, salt, and pepper; cook for 60-90 seconds.
- Add diced tomatoes and their juices; cook until softened, then add reserved liquid and mashed pulses.
- Bring to boil, reduce to simmer for 70-90 minutes, stirring occasionally, adding more water if necessary.
- Stir in cashew cream or coconut milk; simmer for another 10 minutes.
- (Optional) Smoke the dal using the Dhungar method for added flavor.
- Stir in cilantro and lemon juice; season to taste.
- Prepare the tadka by heating vegan butter; add ginger and sauté for 30 seconds, then kasoori methi, garam masala, and red chile powder.
- Pour the tadka over the dal, stir, and serve.
