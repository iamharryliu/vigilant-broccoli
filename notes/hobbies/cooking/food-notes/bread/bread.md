## Bread Lingo

| Term           | Description                                                                                                                                          |
| -------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| autolyse       | Mixing flour and water before adding any other ingredients to build gluten strength.                                                                 |
| levain         | Starter dough.                                                                                                                                       |
| hydration      | The ratio of water to flour in bread making. For example, 80% hydration means 80 grams of water per 100 grams of flour.                              |
| high hydration | High water-to-flour ratio (typically 75%+), creating wetter dough with an airy crumb and crisp crust. Harder to handle but common in artisan breads. |

## Types of Flour

| Flour Type        | Protein Content | Characteristics                          | Common Uses                                 |
| ----------------- | --------------- | ---------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------- |
| All-Purpose Flour | 9–12%           | Versatile, moderate gluten strength      | Quick breads, flatbreads, soft yeast breads |
| Bread Flour       | 12–14%          | Strong gluten development, elastic dough | Baguettes, sourdough, rolls                 |
| Whole Wheat Flour | 13–14%          | Nutty, high fiber, denser texture        | Whole wheat and multigrain breads           |
| Rye Flour         | 5–10%           | Low gluten, dense and earthy flavor      | Rye and pumpernickel breads                 |
