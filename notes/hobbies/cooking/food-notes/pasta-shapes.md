# Pasta Shapes

| Shape                  | Size/Family                   | When to Use                                                                              |
| ---------------------- | ----------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Angel Hair (Capellini) | Long, very thin strand        | Light, thin sauces (olive oil, simple tomato); delicate enough to overcook fast          |
| Spaghetti              | Long, thin strand             | Classic all-purpose; oil-based, tomato, or simple cream sauces                           |
| Bucatini               | Long, thin tube               | Rich, oily sauces (carbonara, amatriciana) — hollow core carries sauce inside the strand |
| Linguine               | Long, flat, narrow            | Seafood and pesto sauces; flat shape holds oil-based sauces well                         |
| Fettuccine             | Long, flat, wide              | Rich cream sauces (alfredo, mushroom cream) — wide surface holds thick sauce             |
| Pappardelle            | Long, flat, very wide         | Hearty meat ragùs and braises; wide ribbon stands up to heavy sauce                      |
| Fusilli                | Short, spiral                 | Chunky vegetable or pesto sauces; grooves trap sauce and bits                            |
| Rotini                 | Short, tight spiral           | Similar to fusilli — pasta salads, chunky sauces                                         |
| Penne                  | Short, ridged tube            | All-purpose baked dishes and chunky tomato sauces; tube holds sauce inside               |
| Rigatoni               | Short, wide ridged tube       | Heavy meat sauces and baked casseroles; large tube for hearty chunks                     |
| Farfalle (Bowtie)      | Short, pinched bow shape      | Light cream sauces and pasta salads; pinched center adds texture                         |
| Orecchiette            | Short, small ear-shaped cup   | Sauces with small bits (broccoli rabe, sausage crumbles) that nestle in the cup          |
| Shells (Conchiglie)    | Short to medium, shell-shaped | Chunky sauces and baked stuffed dishes; shell scoops up sauce and filling                |
| Orzo                   | Small, rice-shaped            | Soups, pilafs, and cold salads; behaves like a grain more than pasta                     |
| Ditalini               | Small, short tube             | Soups (minestrone, pasta e fagioli); small enough to eat by the spoonful                 |
| Lasagna                | Flat, wide sheet              | Layered baked dishes with sauce, cheese, and filling between sheets                      |
