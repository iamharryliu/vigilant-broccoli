# First Aid

## Order of Operations

- Stop Bleeding > Check Breathing > Stabilize environment(hypothermia/heat)
- Do not move the person unless there is an immediate danger (e.g., fire, traffic).
- Keep the person stabilized and comfortable.
- Apply pressure to open wounds to control bleeding.
- If the person is unconscious, check for breathing and pulse.
- If there is no breathing or pulse, begin CPR if you are trained to do so.
- Keep the person warm to prevent shock.
- Reassure the person and keep them calm.
- Call emergency services immediately.

## 1 Severe Bleeding (“Stop the Bleed”)

- **Goal:** Stop blood loss immediately.
- **Actions:**
  - Apply **firm, direct pressure** with cloth/gauze.
  - **Pack deep wounds** tightly, then hold pressure.
  - Use a **tourniquet (limbs only)**:
    - Place 2–3 inches above wound (not on joints).
    - Tighten until bleeding stops (no pulse below).
- **Tips:**
  - Do **not remove** soaked dressings—add more on top.
  - Maintain constant pressure (don’t check repeatedly).
  - Life > limb: prioritize stopping bleeding.

## 2 CPR (Cardiac Arrest)

- **Goal:** Maintain blood flow to brain/heart.
- **Actions:**
  - Check responsiveness and breathing.
  - Call emergency services.
  - Start **chest compressions**:
    - Center of chest
    - Depth: 5–6 cm (2–2.5 in)
    - Rate: 100–120/min
  - If trained: **30 compressions : 2 breaths**
- **Tips:**
  - If unsure: do **hands-only CPR**.
  - Minimize interruptions.
  - Use an AED if available (follow prompts).

## 3 Hypothermia (Too Cold)

- **Signs:** Shivering, confusion, slurred speech, drowsiness
- **Actions:**
  - Move to shelter (dry, out of wind).
  - Remove wet clothing.
  - Insulate with blankets/layers.
  - Warm **core areas first** (chest, neck, head, groin).
- **Tips:**
  - Avoid direct high heat on extremities.
  - Handle gently in severe cases.
  - Give warm, sweet drinks if conscious.

## 4 Heat Exhaustion / Heat Stroke (Too Hot)

- **Heat Exhaustion:** sweating, weakness, dizziness
- **Heat Stroke (critical):** confusion, hot skin, very high temp
- **Actions:**
  - Move to shade/cool area.
  - Remove excess clothing.
  - Cool rapidly:
    - Water + airflow (fan)
    - Ice packs (neck, armpits, groin)
  - Hydrate if conscious.
- **Tips:**
  - Heat stroke = **medical emergency**.
  - Do not force fluids if unconscious.
  - Cooling quickly is the priority.

## Minimal Kit

- Tourniquet (commercial)
- Gauze / pressure bandage
- Gloves
- Space blanket
