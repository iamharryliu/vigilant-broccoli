# Garlicky Sesame Edamame Salad

## Ingredients

- **Edamame**
  - 340g frozen shelled edamame
- **Aromatics**
  - 3 garlic cloves, finely chopped
  - 1 serrano pepper, diced
  - 4 scallions, thinly sliced (save dark greens for garnish)
- **Nuts and Seeds**
  - 1 tbsp white or black sesame seeds
  - 25g unsweetened shredded coconut (optional)
  - 45g dry-roasted peanuts or cashews, roughly chopped
- **Dressing**
  - 1.5 tbsp tahini
  - 1 tbsp agave nectar
  - Zest of 1 medium lime
  - Juice of 1 medium lime
  - 2 tbsp tamari or soy sauce
  - 1 tbsp toasted sesame oil
- **Vegetables**
  - 160g red cabbage, finely grated (optional)
- **Herbs**
  - 8g cilantro leaves and tender stems, chopped
  - Small handful of fresh mint leaves, torn (optional)

## Instructions

- Cook the edamame per package instructions and drain.
- If making a cold salad, rinse the edamame under cold water and pat dry.
- In a bowl, whisk together tahini, agave nectar, lime zest, lime juice, soy sauce, and sesame oil for the dressing; set aside.
- Heat a pan over medium heat, add sesame seeds, coconut, and peanuts, and toast until fragrant, about 2 minutes.
- Add oil, chili pepper, scallions, garlic, and a pinch of salt to the pan; cook until golden brown, about 2-3 minutes.
- Pour the toasted mixture over the edamame and cabbage in a serving bowl.
- Re-whisk the dressing and pour it over the salad; toss to combine.
- Stir in cilantro and mint, and season with salt or lime juice as needed.

## Garnish

- Dark green parts of scallions for garnish
