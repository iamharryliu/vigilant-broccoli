# Driving in Sweden

- Yield to vehicle on the right at uncontrolled intersections.
  - Exceptions:
    - [Home Zone](https://korkortonline.se/en/theory/road-signs/instruction-signs/e9/)
    - [Priority Road](https://korkortonline.se/en/theory/road-signs/priority-signs/b4/)
    - [Junctions](https://korkortonline.se/en/theory/road-signs/warning-signs/a29/)
- [Time indication (T6)](https://korkortonline.se/en/theory/road-signs/additional-panels/t6/)
- [Tyres: summer tyres & winter tyres – driving licence theory](https://korkortonline.se/en/theory/tyres/)
  - 16 April – 30 September - prohibited to use studded tyres, if winter road conditions are not prevailing?
- Solid Line Exceptions:
  - A solid line may be crossed “to enter or exit a property or equivalent area.” (Swedish Road Traffic Ordinance)
- Priority Roads
  - [Priority Road](https://korkortonline.se/en/theory/road-signs/priority-signs/b4/)
  - [Priority Road Ends](https://korkortonline.se/en/theory/road-signs/priority-signs/b5/)
- Prohibitions
  - “If a motor vehicle or a vehicle coupled to this has a load where either side protrudes more than 20 centimetres outside the vehicle, or if the vehicle’s width, including load, exceeds 260 centimetres, [...] the vehicle or a vehicle coupled to this may only be driven on a private road.” (Road Traffic Ordinance)
- Vehicles - [Vehicle Inspection](https://korkortonline.se/en/theory/roadworthiness-test/) - A new car must be inspected for the first time no later than 3 years (36 months) after the month when it was first taken into service.
  The second inspection must be conducted no later than 2 years (24 months) after the month in which the first inspection was conducted.
  Subsequent inspections must be conducted no later than 14 months after the month in which the most recent inspection was conducted.
