# Mathematics Lingo

| Term              | Definition                                                                                                                                                                                                                                  |
| ----------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Vector**        | A mathematical object characterized by both magnitude and direction.                                                                                                                                                                        |
| **Scalar**        | A mathematical object characterized by magnitude only (a single number value).                                                                                                                                                              |
| **Dot Product**   | Product of two vectors (`a · b`) producing a scalar; measures how much they point in the same direction. `a · b = \|a\| \|b\| cos(θ)`; zero when perpendicular.                                                                             |
| **Cross Product** | Product of two 3D vectors (`a × b`) producing a vector perpendicular to both, with magnitude equal to the area of the parallelogram they span. `\|a × b\| = \|a\| \|b\| sin(θ)`; direction follows the right-hand rule; zero when parallel. |
